## Language

**Do**
- Use UK English: organise, colour, programme, specialise.
- Write in active voice: "Our team delivered the system on time."
- Use plain English; explain technical terms where needed.
- Spell out acronyms on first use, then abbreviate — e.g. Mechanical and Electrical (M&E).
- Use "bespoke", "engaging", "expertise" where relevant.
- Use commas, semicolons, and full stops to break thoughts.

**Don't**
- Use US spelling (organize, color, program).
- Use em dashes anywhere.
- Use passive voice ("It was delivered by our team").
- Use "tailored" (use "bespoke"), "exciting" (use "engaging"), or jargon without explanation.
- Use unexplained acronyms.
- Overuse exclamation marks or emojis in formal content.

## Tone

**Do**
- Sound professional, approachable, reliable, forward-thinking, community minded.
- Match tone to channel: precise for tenders, warm for marketing, conversational for social.
- Lead with the client benefit, not internal process.
- Stay calm, factual, and empathetic in sensitive situations.

**Don't**
- Sound corporate-cold or overly casual.
- Overpromise or use marketing hype ("world-class", "best ever").
- Be defensive or sarcastic in any reply.
- Mix tones within a single piece (formal proposal with social-media phrasing).

*Source: [Brand Tone and Voice Guide](https://vitalheating.sharepoint.com/sites/Marketing/_layouts/15/Doc.aspx?sourcedoc=%7BD7232C3A-3FB5-4DDC-BCB7-90AB496D5BFF%7D&file=Vital%20Synergy%20%E2%80%93%20Brand%20Tone%20and%20Voice%20Guide.docx)*

## Images

**Do**
- Get written consent for every identifiable person in marketing imagery.
- Get parental/guardian consent for anyone under 18.
- Store consent forms centrally and refresh when re-using imagery.
- Use imagery showing correct PPE and safe practices on site.
- Remove imagery promptly if consent is withdrawn.

**Don't**
- Publish any identifiable person without a signed consent form.
- Use stock photos that misrepresent our team or work.
- Show unsafe practices, missing PPE, or hazards.
- Re-use consented imagery outside the agreed scope or timeframe.
- Use low-quality, blurred, or out-of-context images.

## Social media

**Do**
- Submit every post for Marketing approval before publishing.
- Respond to comments and DMs within one business day.
- Escalate complaints, incidents, or sensitive topics to the Marketing lead.
- Maintain a content calendar aligned with the Marketing Strategy.
- Use approved hashtags only.
- Keep 2FA enabled on all accounts.

**Don't**
- Post outside the approval workflow.
- Engage in political, religious, or contentious debate.
- Reply when angry, defensive, or unsure — escalate.
- Share client information without written approval.
- Share account passwords; use the approved password manager.
- Leave 2FA disabled on any account.

*Source: [Social Media SOPs](https://vitalheating.sharepoint.com/sites/Marketing/_layouts/15/Doc.aspx?sourcedoc=%7BDABC5069-0986-47AA-9398-583D12FE3542%7D&file=Social%20Media%20SOPs.docx)*
